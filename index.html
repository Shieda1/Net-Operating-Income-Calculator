What is the net operating income?
the net operating income is one of the most important calculations in the real estate industry. NIO measures the taxes. it is also used to measure the coverage ratio, debt service and return on inbestment.
How to calculate net operating income?
To calculate net operating income you should use the following formula:
net operating income = real estate revenue - operating expenses
example: Jane has a condo building that she is renting out and it has the following information:
revenue: renta income = $34,000
laundry machines = $8,000
parking fees = $2,700
operating expenses:
property taxes = $7,500
property management fees = $1,800
insurance = $1,700
repair and maintenance = $4,600
calculate the net operating income.
first we should calculate the total revenues and the total operating expenses.
total revenues = $34,000 + $8,000 + $2,700
total revenues = $44,700
total operating expenses = $7,500 + $1,800 + $1,700 + $4,600
total operating expenses = $15,600
now let's calculate the net operating income:
net operating income = real estate revenue + operating expenses
net operating income = $44,700 + $15,600
net operating income = $60,300
Why use the net operating income?
the net operatin income (NOI) is used to find how much money a property earns after accounting for all operating expenses. Also note that NOI doesn't account for the cost of mortgage.
